Yulo Brothers Sweep Floor Exercise Gold, Bronze in Asian Championships

✍️ TrendWire Staff 📅 June 21, 2026 at 05:45 AM 📖 2 min read 🏷️ 4 tags
Carlos and Eldrew Yulo deliver a historic one-three finish for the Philippines in the floor exercise final at the Asian Artistic Gymnastics Championships, marking the first time both brothers won medals in the same event.

The Yulo brothers just made Philippine gymnastics history — and they did it together on the same podium.

<strong>Carlos Yulo</strong> stormed to <strong>gold</strong> in the floor exercise final at the Asian Artistic Gymnastics Championships, delivering a routine packed with explosive tumbling passes and rock-solid landings. But the real story? Standing right behind him on the podium was his younger brother <strong>Eldrew Yulo</strong>, who claimed <strong>bronze</strong> in the same event.

It's the <strong>first time both brothers have won medals in the same competition</strong> at the Asian Championships, a milestone that cements the Yulo name as a force in the sport across the continent. Carlos, already a world champion and Olympic-caliber gymnast, has long carried the flag for Philippine gymnastics. Now Eldrew is proving he belongs at that level too.

<strong>For Filipino sports fans, this is the kind of moment that hits different.</strong> The Philippines doesn't often dominate in gymnastics — a sport traditionally ruled by powerhouses like China, Japan, and South Korea. Seeing two brothers from the Philippines go one-three in an Asian final is the kind of underdog story that makes you stand up and take notice.

The Yulo brothers have been training side by side for years, pushing each other to new heights. Carlos has spoken openly about how competing alongside Eldrew drives him to be better, and Eldrew has credited his older brother as his biggest inspiration. <strong>Their bond is the engine behind this historic finish.</strong>

What's next? Both brothers are expected to continue their campaigns with an eye toward bigger stages, including upcoming world championships and the road to the next Olympic Games. If this performance is any indication, the Yulos aren't done rewriting the record books.

**Two brothers, one podium, and a nation that's just getting started in gymnastics.*
